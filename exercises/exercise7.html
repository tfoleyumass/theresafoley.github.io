Projects

  • Ethical Computing Article

    A magazine article defining ethical computing in a global context. This piece explores the principles of ethical computing, highlighting issues such as data privacy, algorithmic bias, and responsible AI development. Using textual evidence, real-world examples, and hyperlinks, the article aims to educate readers on best practices for ensuring technology serves society ethically and fairly.

  • Microsoft Word Guide for Creative Writing Majors

    A step-by-step guide on how to use Microsoft Word for writing a book, tailored specifically for creative writing majors. This guide covers essential tools such as formatting manuscripts, utilizing styles for efficient editing, tracking changes for collaborative writing, and organizing chapters with the navigation pane. It also provides tips on managing citations, inserting footnotes, and exporting final drafts for submission to publishers or agents.

  • Expanding Literacy Programs at Wellesley Public Library: A Feasibility Study

    This feasibility study explores securing funding to expand literacy programs at the Wellesley Public Library, encouraging students to develop a love for reading. The project aims to enhance existing programs through reading workshops, diverse book collections, and interactive events. Research will assess literacy rates, funding sources, and program effectiveness while addressing potential challenges like staffing and participation.

  • The Crown Conspiracy

    A fantasy novel exploring themes of power, deception, and political intrigue. Set in a kingdom on the brink of war, the story follows a young noble who uncovers a conspiracy threatening the royal family. As alliances shift and betrayals unfold, the protagonist must navigate a treacherous web of secrets to prevent the collapse of the empire. The novel combines elements of magic, espionage, and courtly drama, making it an immersive tale of strategy and survival.
